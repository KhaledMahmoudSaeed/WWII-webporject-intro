The Eastern Front

The Soviet Union bore the brunt of Nazi Germany's military might, with the Eastern Front becoming the largest and bloodiest theater of war in history. After initial devastating losses in 1941, the Soviet military reorganized and began to push back, culminating in the decisive victory at Stalingrad - often considered the turning point of the entire war.

Soviet industry, despite losing much of its western territories, managed to outproduce Germany in key areas like tank production. The T-34 tank became emblematic of Soviet technological and industrial achievements during the war. By 1945, the Red Army had advanced from Stalingrad all the way to Berlin, capturing the German capital and effectively ending the war in Europe.
