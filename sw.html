Neutrality Under Pressure

Switzerland maintained its neutrality through a combination of military preparedness, geographic advantages, and economic concessions to both sides. The Swiss Army, under General Henri Guisan, developed the National Redoubt strategy - a plan to retreat into the Alpine mountains if invaded, making conquest prohibitively costly for any aggressor.

Swiss banks handled financial transactions for both Allied and Axis powers, leading to postwar controversies about Nazi gold. At the same time, Switzerland served as an important diplomatic hub and protected thousands of refugees, though its refugee policies became more restrictive as the war progressed. The International Red Cross, based in Geneva, played a crucial humanitarian role throughout the conflict.
