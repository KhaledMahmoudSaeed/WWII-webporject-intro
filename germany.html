GERMAN PERSPECTIVE

The Third Reich's military innovations and strategic campaigns during World War II

Blitzkrieg Tactics

Pioneered rapid, coordinated attacks combining air power and armored units to overwhelm enemies quickly.

Technological Advancements

Developed advanced weapons including the V-2 rocket, jet aircraft, and improved tank designs.

European Conquest

By 1941, Germany controlled or allied with most of continental Europe through military campaigns.

Military Strategy

The German military (Wehrmacht) emphasized mobility, initiative, and decentralized decision-making through the Auftragstaktik (mission-type tactics) system. This allowed for rapid adaptation to battlefield conditions and often outmaneuvered more rigid Allied command structures in the early war years.

The Luftwaffe provided close air support that was integral to Blitzkrieg success, while the Kriegsmarine focused on U-boat warfare to disrupt Allied supply lines across the Atlantic.
